An equation in the form below is what is called a nonhomogenous 2nd order linear differential equation:

(1)

L[y]=y′′+p(t)y′+q(t)y=g(t) L[y] = y'' + p(t)y' + q(t)y = g(t) L[y]=y′′+p(t)y′+q(t)y=g(t)

(2)

L[y]=y′′+p(t)y′+q(t)y=0 L[y] = y'' + p(t)y' + q(t)y = 0 L[y]=y′′+p(t)y′+q(t)y=0

If you have two solutions to the nonhomogenous differential equation, then you can find the fundamental solution to the corresponding homogenous differential equation.

Summary of the above

With all of the work described above, we arrive at the general way we solve nonhomogenous differential equations in the form of equation (1):

  1. Find the general solution c1y1+c2y2c_1y_1 + c_2y_2c1​y1​+c2​y2​ of the corresponding homogenous equation (2). This solution is called the complementary solution, denoted as yc(t)y_c(t)yc​(t).
  2. Find any solution Y(t)Y(t)Y(t) of the nonhomogenous equation (1). This solution is called the particular solution.
  3. Form the sum of the functions from 1 and 2 to satsify the general form written above

The ways of finding yc(t)y_c(t)yc​(t) are general with the way to solve homogenous equations, so that will not be discussed here. Instead, we focus on the nonhomogenous solution, step 2.

2. Finding the nonhomogenous solution, Y(t)Y(t)Y(t)

The two methods for finding nonhomogenous solutions mentioned here are the following:

  1. The method of undetermined coefficients (Section 3.5)
  2. The method of variation of parameters (Section 3.6)

The Method of Undetermined Coefficents

The method of undetermined coefficents is mostly a "guess-and-check" type of solution, where to find Y(t)Y(t)Y(t), we make an assumption on the general form for Y(t)Y(t)Y(t) and leave the coefficents unspecified. We then substitute this Y(t)Y(t)Y(t) into the given nonhomogenous equation in the form of equation (1) and attempt to determine the coefficents that satisfy the equation. If we cannot find coefficents that satisfy the equation, then we must find a new general form for Y(t)Y(t)Y(t) and try again.

This method uses mostly brute force and domain knowledge to find a solution, so it's restricted to equations that have relatively simple makeup.
