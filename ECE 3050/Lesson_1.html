If it is infinite, the signal is a power signal. !!! note: Note Energy Signal has zero power, power signal has infinite energy !!! example: Sinusoid Signal Is a sinusoid signal a power signal or energy signal? $$ \int^\pi_0 \sin^2{t} dt = \int^\pi_0 \frac{1+\sin{2t}}{2} dt = \int^\pi_0 \frac{1}{2}\ dt + \int^\pi_0 \frac{\sin{2t}}{2} dt = \frac{\pi}{2} $$ We take this result and sum it up across the entire domain, finding the following. $$ \int^\inf_{-\inf} \sin^2{t} dt = \sum^\inf_{k=-\inf} \int^{(k+1)\pi}_{k\pi} \sin^2{t} dt = \sum^\inf_{k=-\inf} \frac{pi}{2} = \inf $$ A sinusoid has infinite energy in it's signal. This is a power signal, but not an energy signal, as there are nonzero values at all points in time (except at $x=k\pi$). !!! example: Exponential Signal Is an exponential signal a power signal or energy signal? $$ x(t) = e^{-2t} $$ $$ E = \int^\inf_{-\inf} e^{-2t} dt = \frac{e^{-2t}}{ -2}\bigg|^\inf_0 = \frac{1}{2} $$ An exponential signal has a finite energy value.
