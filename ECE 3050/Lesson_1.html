**Lesson 1** $$ \def\R{{\mathbb{R}}} \def\Z{{\mathbb{Z}}} \def\N{{\mathbb{N}}} \newcommand{\Zn}[1]{\mathbb{Z}_{\\#1}} $$ # Signals ## Important Signals Common vocabulary and notation for frequently encountered signals: * Real exponential * Sinusoid * Exponentially damped sinusoid * Step * Impulse * Ramp * Pulse ## Complex Representations $$ \text{Imaginary Number Definition} \\ j = \sqrt{-1} \\ j^2 = -1 \\ $$ $$ \text{Euler's Identity} \\ e^{j\theta} = \cos{\theta} + j\sin{\theta} \\ $$ $$ \text{Cartesian Form} \\ \text{Given $x$,$y$ are in $\R$:} \\ z = x + jy\\ $$ ## Real-valued Exponental Signals A decaying signal that starts at some value $c$ and decays at some rate $a$. $$ x(t) = \begin{cases} ce^{-at} & t \ge 0 \\ 0 & t \lt 0 \\ \end{cases} $$ Viewing the signal as an entire object, we can say the signal is constant with respect to time ![Continuous Real-valued Exponential Signal](Lesson_1/RV_exp.png) The discrete variant is slightly different: $$ x[n] = \begin{cases} cr^n & n \gt 0 \\ 0 & n \lt 0 \end{cases} $$ ![Discrete Real-valued Exponential Signal](Lesson_1/RV_exp_samp.png)
